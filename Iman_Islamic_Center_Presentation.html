Slide 2: Context

Why a New Site?

  • Existing site was hard to read on phones.
  • Forms were only downloadable as DOCX – many users could not open them.
  • Navigation was cluttered; important links were hidden.
Speaker Note:
“People needed a clean, mobile‑friendly experience and easy access to all the community forms.”
Slide 3: Objectives

Main Goals

  • Responsive design – works on desktop, tablet, and phone.
  • Clear navigation – intuitive dropdown menus for Services, Forms, etc.
  • Universal form access – switch all downloadable forms to PDF.
  • Premium look – modern colors, smooth animations, and consistent fonts.
Speaker Note:
“Four simple goals guided every change we made.”
Slide 4: Technology

Tech Stack (at a glance)

  • HTML – page structure.
  • JavaScript (React) – interactive components (navbar, forms).
  • CSS – custom styling, glass‑morphism, hover effects.
  • Vite – fast development server.
Speaker Note:
“We kept the stack lightweight: pure React with Vite, no heavy frameworks, which makes the site fast and easy to maintain.”
Slide 5: Design Highlight

Navigation Redesign

  • Centered top bar with two “pill” frames:
    • Language switcher.
    • Social‑media icons (spaced 200 px apart).
  • Dropdown menus for Services, Forms, About Us, etc.
  • Phone/Contact pod moved closer to the “Donate” button.
Speaker Note:
“The new navbar looks like a premium app bar – the language selector and social icons are clearly separated, and the contact info is now right next to the Donate button.”
Slide 6: Key Feature

Forms – From DOCX to PDF

  • All files placed in public/forms folder.
  • Links updated to .pdf extensions.
  • Example: “Become Board Member” now downloads board‑of‑directors‑member‑work‑form.pdf.
  • PDFs open directly in browsers on any device.
Speaker Note:
“This change solves the biggest pain point – users can view or print a form instantly, even on a phone.”
Slide 7: Usability

Shortened Form Names

  • Long names trimmed for the dropdown menu:
    • “Membership Forms” → Membership
    • “Donation Forms” → Donations
    • “Become a Board Member” → Board Member
  • Arabic titles also shortened.
Speaker Note:
“Short names keep the menu tidy and prevent text from being cut off.”
Slide 8: Aesthetics

Visual Polish (Premium Feel)

  • Color palette: deep blues with vibrant accents.
  • Glass‑morphism on the top bar – subtle background blur.
  • Micro‑animations: hover effects on links, smooth dropdown transitions.
  • Responsive typography: larger headings, balanced body text.
Speaker Note:
“These visual tweaks give the site a modern, trustworthy look while staying accessible.”
Slide 9: Demonstration

Demo (Live Walk‑through)

  • Show the homepage on a desktop screen.
  • Resize to a mobile view – notice the hamburger menu and stacked layout.
  • Click Forms → Membership → Board Member – PDF opens instantly.
  • Switch language – layout stays consistent.
Speaker Note:

Slide 10: Future Steps

Future Enhancements

  • Online form submission (no download needed).
  • Multilingual content – full RTL support for Arabic.
  • Accessibility audit – ARIA labels, keyboard navigation.
  • Analytics – track which forms are most downloaded.
Speaker Note:
“We have a roadmap for more interactivity and deeper accessibility.”
Slide 11: Conclusion

Impact & Benefits

  • Better user experience – faster access, clear navigation.
  • Higher engagement – more people can fill out and submit forms.
  • Reduced support calls – fewer questions about opening files.
  • Modern brand image – aligns with the Center’s mission of community outreach.
Speaker Note:
“The site now truly serves the community’s needs and reflects the Center’s values.”
